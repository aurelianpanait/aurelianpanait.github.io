About

I am a Master's student in Energy at MINES Paris – PSL, specialising in renewable energy integration and energy efficiency. I hold a Bachelor's degree in Energy Engineering from the National University of Science and Technology POLITEHNICA of Bucharest, where I graduated in the top 10% of my class (final grade: 85.7%). My Bachelor's thesis, "Floating Photovoltaic Panels: Assessing the Potential, Advantages, and Challenges of Capturing Solar Energy on Water Surfaces", received a grade of 98% (top 1%) and included the development of a Tilted Global Solar Irradiance model for floating PV analysis.

Following this, I was admitted to the Master's programme in Hydroinformatics and Water Engineering at POLITEHNICA Bucharest, Faculty of Energy Engineering, where I achieved a first-semester grade of 94% and conducted research on the influence of floating photovoltaic panels on water evaporation. During the second semester, I was selected through a competitive process for an Erasmus exchange at MINES Paris – PSL, where I was subsequently admitted as a full-time M2 student in the highly selective Energy Master's programme.

I have completed internships in commodities and energy trading, focusing on market analysis, forecasting, and risk assessment. My interests span financial and energy markets, data-driven decision making, and the energy transition.
